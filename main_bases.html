> Klein-Honningstal - "Small Valley of Honningen" by AlbertAllrad.

Being one of the more recent additions to the Kaida-area, Klein-Honningstal rose from one small house in the forest covering the gate to the 1-Year-Area.
Back then there were no plans to ever grow to the scale it has reached today.

Being inspired of IRL German castle towns like Meissen and Eisenach, the town still is growing to this day.
It consists of multiple levels:

The first area is made up of the town's top carpenters, blacksmiths, brewers and more.
Built around the original home of today's Castle Lord, it is the first stop for visitors and messengers from the other areas.
It also contains the townhall.

The Second area Wraps around the inner Walls and is purely residential buildings (or so the castle's army would have you believe).
It is secured by a high wall to the outside and has a secret emergency passage to the primary castle area, giving it's residents the best possible chances of survival during a siege.

At the peaks of the mountains overlooking the 1-Year-Area you can find the main Castle Buildings: To the North the Burgfried (The Donjon) with the Lord's residence and stables.
South of it is the secondary keep, acting as a central storage hub, also featuring most of the town's trade commerce.
It is directly connected to Klein-Honningtal's numerous farms, providing an immediate supply of goods to the town and it's people.

Klein-Honningstal is ever-expanding and invites Visitors and Travellers alike to stay a few nights, trade with the locals, and enjoy the rustic German way of life which the town is famous for.

> HollowMoon by ArchedRocketeer.

HollowMoon, the sea-side city is a small but growing city, a citadel among the various settlements and villages.
Its lively streets are lined with colorful stalls, bustling shops, and towering municipal buildings.
The docks are busy with various ships, from small fishing boats to old cargo sailing ships, you'll always find work on the piers leading into HollowMoon.

The land of HollowMoon is lively and wild, seemingly filled with magic as HollowMoon expands, whether that may be to the skies, land, or sea!
If there's a place you need to go then fear not!
The people of HollowMoon are happy to help.

> Barad-dur - "The Dark Tower of Sauron" by ZakZNinja.

A recreation of Sauron's fortress from the Lord of the Rings movie trilogy.
Barad-Dur began construction on the Project-Kaida server shortly after the completion of the Pyramid,
both to rival its presence and to create a second monolith visible from spawn.
It is part of the land and faction of "Mordor" accessible by crossing the viaduct connecting to the spawn island, marked by the "Black Gate."

Upon entering Mordor, you will see a moat of lava surrounding Barad-Dur, protecting it from invaders and feeding the forges in the tower's lower chambers.
This moat is fuelled by only the most fearsome of fires,
harvested from "Mount Doom," which resides in the Nether - accessible through the sandstone-encased portal beside the viaduct.

The fortress's outer gate is guarded by a door of lava that opens into the entrance hall, from which all detailed interior floors can be accessed.
These currently include:
- a vast storeroom for stockpiling the spoils of war and industry,
- a throne room used to give commands and display authority and power,
- personal bedchambers, as even Sauron's eyes must rest,
- a library containing powerful and dark magical knowledge,
along with one of the few remaining Palantiri — bewitched seeing stones that allow strong-willed users to communicate and gaze over long distances.

At the summit of Barad-Dur, the "Eye of Sauron" uses the power of the Palantiri and the One Ring to watch over the server and ensure that no other factions rise against Mordor.
This also allows the Dark Lord to track the movements of the Orcs and Nazgul — his armies and servants — keeping them bound to his rule.

In contrast to the scorched land of Mordor, the surrounding area is very fertile due to nutrient-rich volcanic soil.
This has led to the prosperity of nearby woodlands and an oasis.
These natural resources are used to feed the armies and fuel the war machine (but no need to worry about that).

If you decide to simply walk into Mordor, the Black Gate is always open to those who wish to gaze upon the magnificence of the dark fortress from within its grounds.

Etymology: Barad-dur means "Dark Fortress" in Sindarin, from barad ("fortress/tower") and dur ("dark").
Sindarin being the most common variant of the Elven tongue in the Third Age.
